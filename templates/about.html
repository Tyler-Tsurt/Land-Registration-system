Ministry of Lands and Natural Resources

The Ministry of Lands and Natural Resources is responsible for land administration, forest management, and climate change programmes coordination in Zambia.

About the Ministry

The Ministry was realigned in 2016 through Government Gazette Number 836 of 2016. The Ministry consists of eight (8) departments:

  • Human Resource and Administration
  • Lands Department
  • Survey Department
  • Lands and Deeds Registry
  • Forestry Department
  • Climate Change and Natural Resource Management
  • Finance and Planning
  • Information Department

Statutory Bodies: The Ministry is responsible for the Zambia Forestry College, Lands Tribunal, and Survey Control Board.

Land Registry System

Zambia operates two main land registries: Lusaka and Ndola. These registries are responsible for registering documents related to granting, conveying, or transferring land within timeframes stipulated by the Lands and Deeds Registry Act.

Benefits of Land Registration:

  • Provides conclusive evidence of land ownership
  • Aids in dispute resolution and conflict management
  • Enables land to be used as security for credit facilities
  • Promotes transparency in land transactions
  • Supports economic development through secure property rights

Ndola City Council

The local authority serving the City of Ndola in the Copperbelt Province

About Ndola

Ndola is the third largest city in Zambia and the capital of the Copperbelt Province. The city is a major industrial and commercial center, playing a crucial role in land administration and development planning in the region.

Role in Land Administration:

  • Processing land applications within Ndola municipal boundaries
  • Coordinating with the Ministry of Lands for title deed issuance
  • Managing urban planning and land use regulations
  • Maintaining cadastral records and survey maps
  • Facilitating public consultations on land matters
