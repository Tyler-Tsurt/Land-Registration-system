About Our System

Introduction to Ndola Land System

Land administration is a critical pillar in national development, economic stability, and social harmony. In Zambia, especially in highly developing areas like Ndola, land ownership disputes have become increasingly prevalent. This project proposes the development of a digital land registry system for Ndola that leverages Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Geographic Information Systems (GIS). The system is designed to automatically detect conflicts in land ownership records and provide visual mapping of land parcels. This dual-layered approach enhances transparency, improves spatial validation, and strengthens the integrity of land data management at the district level.

Our Aim

The primary aim of this study is to develop a secure, intelligent, and locally deployable land registry system for Ndola, Zambia, that utilizes artificial intelligence to detect potential ownership conflicts and data anomalies. The system is intended to enhance transparency, reduce manual errors, and support faster resolution of land-related disputes by integrating real-time validation tools into the digital registration process.

Significance of Our Work

This research offers practical contributions to Zambia's digital transformation agenda by addressing localized issues in land governance through innovative technology. By demonstrating how AI can be operationalized within a local registry context, the system paves the way for national-scale implementation. It also contributes to academic discourse on the application of machine learning and digital governance in Sub-Saharan Africa.
